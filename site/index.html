Parfait notices when a meeting starts, records both sides, transcribes with named speakers, and writes notes into your own templates.

Transcribed and summarized entirely on your Mac. Free and open source.

View on GitHub How to install ↓

Requires macOS 26 (Tahoe) on Apple Silicon, with Apple Intelligence enabled.

Install

git clone https://github.com/conrad-vanl/parfait.git
cd parfait
make install        # builds Parfait.app into /Applications
open /Applications/Parfait.app

About two minutes, no App Store or developer account. make install signs the app so mic and system-audio permissions survive rebuilds.

What it is

Parfait is an open-source alternative to Granola: a menu-bar app that records your mic and everyone else's audio, transcribes and separates speakers, and writes notes into a template you control — no bot in your call, no server to reach.

How it works

  1. 1

    Detects the meeting. Notices your mic come into use by a call app and offers to record, or starts automatically.

  2. 2

    Records both sides. Your mic through AVAudioEngine; everyone else through a Core Audio system-audio tap — no virtual driver, no bot.

  3. 3

    Transcribes and names speakers. SpeechAnalyzer timestamps the transcript; FluidAudio tells voices apart; calendar attendees become name suggestions.

  4. 4

    Writes your notes. Apple Intelligence summarizes into an editable template — Meeting Notes, 1-on-1, Interview, or your own.

Publish

When a meeting's worth sharing, Parfait renders notes and transcript into one self-contained page, then publishes it as a secret gist on your own GitHub. A notes.parfait.to link serves it back rendered, shareable with anyone; delete the gist and the link goes dark (cached copies clear within a day). Or skip publishing: preview in-browser or export the HTML, nothing leaving your Mac.

Chat with your meetings

Ask a meeting "what did we decide?" — the Chat tab opens Claude Desktop with that meeting loaded through Parfait's MCP connector (the protocol AI tools use to read local data), prompt pre-filled. "Ask your meetings" does the same across your whole library. Point Claude Code at the same server:

claude mcp add parfait -s user -- "/Applications/Parfait.app/Contents/MacOS/Parfait" --mcp

The MCP server runs locally over your meeting folder — it reads your meetings and manages your note templates, and nothing leaves your Mac but what the model reads through it.

Privacy

Recording, transcription, diarization, and summarization happen on your Mac: SpeechAnalyzer (Apple's on-device speech engine) transcribes, a small third-party model (FluidAudio) separates speakers, and Apple Intelligence's FoundationModels summarizes. Parfait makes one network call, downloading those models once. Everything else — Claude Desktop for chat, your claude CLI for long meetings, GitHub for publishing — runs through your own signed-in apps, on your own accounts.

Parfait has no backend of its own. The one piece the project runs is notes.parfait.to, and only a page you explicitly publish ever reaches it: it fetches your gist to render the shared link and caches it briefly, never storing anything you didn't choose to share.

MIT-licensed, about 5,800 lines of Swift: recording in Sources/Parfait/Audio, transcription and diarization in Sources/Parfait/Transcription, summaries in Sources/Parfait/Intelligence.

Granola runs a bot in your meeting and sends your audio to a server. Parfait doesn't.
