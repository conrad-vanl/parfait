Install

A signed .app you can drag straight into Applications is coming soon. For now, building from source is the way to try it.

git clone https://github.com/conrad-vanl/parfait.git
cd parfait
make install        # builds Parfait.app into /Applications
open /Applications/Parfait.app

About two minutes, and you don't need the App Store or a developer account.

✦ Or just ask Claude Code to do it: Install https://github.com/conrad-vanl/parfait.git ↗

How it works

  1. 1

    Sees the meeting coming. A card appears when a calendar meeting is about to start — Join & record — and when any app picks up your mic, Parfait offers to record.

  2. 2

    Records both sides. Your microphone and everyone else's audio, taken straight from the system. No virtual driver, no bot in your call.

  3. 3

    Names the speakers. Voices are told apart on your Mac, every line is timestamped, and the people on the calendar invite are offered as names.

  4. 4

    Writes your notes in seconds. A draft streams in the moment you stop, then quietly improves — shaped by your own editable templates: Meeting Notes, a 1-on-1, an interview.

  5. 5

    Pulls out the follow-ups. Every commitment lands in the queue you saw above — edit, dismiss, or hand to Claude.

Your assistant — before, during, after

Parfait ships as a Claude plugin: one install connects your meeting library to Claude Desktop and Claude Code, and adds three skills. Scoop briefs you before a meeting — what you owe these people, what they owe you. Followups works the queue when the meeting ends. Digest rolls up your week: decisions made, commitments aging, questions still open. And during a call, Ask Claude live answers from the running transcript. Claude reads your meetings on demand, through your own account — no sync step, no second copy of your library on someone's server.

claude plugin marketplace add conrad-vanl/parfait
claude plugin install parfait@parfait

Or click Install plugin inside Parfait and it's done for you.

Publish

When a meeting is worth sharing, Create shareable link turns it into a single page at a short notes.parfait.to address, backed by a secret gist on your own GitHub — include the transcript or share just the notes. Open follow-ups ride along, and anyone with the link can hand one to Claude straight from the page, no Parfait needed. Deleting the gist takes the page down (caches can keep serving it for up to a day); to keep things fully private, preview in your browser or export the HTML and nothing leaves your Mac.

Privacy

Recording, transcription, and telling voices apart always happen on your Mac, using Apple's built-in speech engine plus a small set of open voice models (~22 MB, downloaded once — the only thing Parfait ever downloads). Notes are written on-device by Apple Intelligence, or by Claude through your own account — Claude is preferred by default when Claude Code is installed, and one Settings toggle keeps notes on-device, with Claude stepping in only when a meeting is too long for the local model. Every meeting is plain files in a folder: greppable, backupable, yours.

Parfait has no backend and no accounts. The one thing the project runs is notes.parfait.to, and it only ever sees pages you chose to publish.
