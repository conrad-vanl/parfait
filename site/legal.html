What this service is

notes.parfait.to serves rendered copies of meeting-notes pages created with Parfait. The underlying content lives in a secret gist on the publisher's own GitHub account; this service fetches that gist and serves it as a viewable page. It stores nothing itself beyond short-lived caches.

Acceptable use

The only permitted use is serving pages generated by Parfait's export pipeline. Content served through this service must not include:

  • phishing or social engineering of any kind — including pages whose only interactive element is a link, phone number, or email designed to mislead;
  • malware, exploits, or links that lead to either;
  • impersonation of a person, company, or service;
  • content that is illegal where it is published or viewed;
  • spam, SEO schemes, or use of this service as a generic CDN or file host.

Requests for content that was not generated by Parfait are refused automatically. That technical filter is not the boundary of this policy — content that passes the filter but violates the list above is still prohibited.

Enforcement and takedowns

Publishers or individual pages may be blocked at any time, without notice, at the operator's sole discretion. Because the source content lives in the publisher's own gist, deleting the gist removes the source immediately; cached copies of the rendered page expire within a day (browsers within an hour), and can be purged sooner.

Reporting abuse

Report an abusive link by opening an abuse report on GitHub. Reports are reviewed on a best-effort, usually same-day basis.

No warranty

This is a free service for an open-source project, provided as-is, with no uptime or availability guarantees. It may change or be discontinued at any time; published gists and locally exported HTML are unaffected by either.
